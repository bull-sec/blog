I'm personally not a fan of painting my minis using some of the little trends such as "non-metallic metal" or "synthwave", and as much as seeing a base painted in Goblin Green hits me right in the nostalgia, it's 2026, we've moved on. That being said, I can still appreciate great painting when I see it, and I'm tempted to start a little "Things I found on the internet" series for things like non-metalic metal or super bright synthwave minis that I spot on my travels.

Nah, super bright and super technical have their places, and their appreciators. I'm all about the grimdark. The gritty and the grimy. The muddy and the ugly. And as I've gotten older I've found myself more and more drawn towards the darkness as it were. For one, if you combine "grimdark" techniques with contrast paints and some elements of slapchop then you can get really good looking minis painted up relatively quickly.
